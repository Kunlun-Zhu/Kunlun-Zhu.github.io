<p class="kz-intro">
Hello! I am <b>Kunlun Zhu</b>, a Computer Science Ph.D. student at the
<b>University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC)</b>, advised by
<a href="https://blender.cs.illinois.edu/hengji.html">Prof. Heng Ji</a> at
<a href="https://blender.cs.illinois.edu/index.html">BlenderLab</a>. I completed my
M.S. in Computer Science at UIUC, advised by
<a href="https://cs.stanford.edu/people/jiaxuan/">Prof. Jiaxuan You</a> and Prof. Heng Ji.
I am currently a Research Intern at <b>Apodex AI</b> (since June 2026), working on
<b>Agentic AI</b> and <b>Agents for Science</b>.
</p>

<p>
I also collaborate closely with <a href="https://www.james-zou.com/">Prof. James Zou</a>
at Stanford University, where I was a visiting student in 2025. Earlier, I spent two years
as a Research Assistant in the Natural Language Processing group at Tsinghua University and
as an Algorithm Engineer at ModelBest Inc., working with
<a href="https://lzy.thunlp.org/">Prof. Zhiyuan Liu</a>, and I am a proud member of
<a href="https://www.openbmb.cn/en/about-us">OpenBMB</a>. Before that, I was a Research
Assistant in the Graph Team at Mila – Quebec AI Institute with
<a href="https://jian-tang.com/">Prof. Jian Tang</a>, and a research intern at Carnegie
Mellon University's Robotics Institute with
<a href="https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~sycara/">Prof. Katia Sycara</a>.
</p>

<h2 class="kz-h2"><span class="kz-emoji">🧭</span> Research Interests</h2>

<p>My research builds and studies <b>agentic systems powered by large language models</b>,
with the long-term goal of AI that can reliably reason, collaborate, and accelerate
scientific discovery. My current focus areas:</p>

<div class="kz-tags">
  <span>Agents for Science</span>
  <span>Multi-Agent Systems</span>
  <span>Agent Post-Training (RL &amp; Agent Tuning)</span>
  <span>Tool Learning &amp; Planning</span>
  <span>Agent Safety &amp; Evaluation</span>
  <span>Embodied Agents</span>
</div>
